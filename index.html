The shield is gone. The paperwork is the new defense.

In Montgomery v. Caribe Transport, the Court held 9–0 that state negligent‑selection claims against brokers are no longer preempted. One crash involving a carrier you should have flagged can become a lawsuit you can’t get dismissed — and your lack of documentation becomes the plaintiff’s exhibit A.

The exposure

Negligent‑selection suits proceed to discovery. Plaintiffs subpoena what you knew about the carrier the day you tendered.

The defense

A contemporaneous, tamper‑evident record showing you applied a written carrier‑selection policy — per load, timestamped.
